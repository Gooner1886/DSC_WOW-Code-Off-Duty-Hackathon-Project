Karnataka showcases Mysore region as MSME, Tourism Destination

1. High Manufacturing share


  • Mysore has 16.% Manufacturing share in its district income
  • Mysore is the least agricultural based district in Karnataka
  • Manufacturing industries include hand woven Khadi, exquisite Silk Sarees, Inlay Rosewood Furniture and Incense Sticks industries to name a select few

2. Most Developed District in Karnataka

  • Out Of the six districts, Mysore district is relatively better developed and contributes 4.4 per cent to the state GSDP
  • The remaining five districts contribute 7.9 per cent to Karnataka GSDP
  • Mysore is fairly well placed in Human Development Index rankings

3. Best Industrial Base in Karnataka


  • The bulk of the large and medium industries, 42 out of 60, are in Mysore district
  • Even in the case of small-scale industrial (SSI) units, of the total 66,458 SSI units in the region (as on March 31), Mysore with 23,663 units (36 per cent)

4. Efficient Interconnectivity between Cities

  • Road connectivity between Mysore to Mangalore connects to the port (New Mangalore Port) for the development of industry and tourism in this region.
  • Mysore airport plays a major role in improving the tourist inflow in this region, along with adding value to the IT-BT and other industries.
  • Railways – Track doubling works have been completed and railway electrification surveys are conducted too

5. Access to Markets


  • Mysore is a shopper’s delight, seamlessly blending old-world charm with modern trends. You can get lost in the vibrant bazaars and pick up local handicrafts such as wooden dolls and incense sticks, or head to a swanky new mall for the latest brands and eateries.
  • Markets act as a hub for rapidly integrating cities in Karnataka
  • Mysuru accounts to be one of the few traditional places in the country, where you can buy straight from the source, which fruitfully guarantees authentic products at genuine prices.
